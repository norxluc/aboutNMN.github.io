About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in Integrative Physiology at the University of Colorado Boulder. My research explores the neuromechanics of locomotion and neurorehabilitation strategies using technologies like Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation, motion capture, and intermittent hypoxia.

I have prior experience in clinical biomechanics, ocular motor function, and human performance labs. I am passionate about bridging neuroscience and rehabilitation to enhance motor recovery in clinical populations.

Research Interests

  • Sensorimotor neuroplasticity and cortical excitability
  • Locomotor adaptation and gait rehabilitation
  • Split-belt treadmill paradigms
  • Neural stimulation techniques and biomechanics
  • Dual-task interference and fall prevention
